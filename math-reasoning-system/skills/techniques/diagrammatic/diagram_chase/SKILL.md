# Skill: Diagram Chase and Diagrammatic Reasoning

**Pre-condition:** Apply this skill when the problem contains commutative diagrams, exact sequences, maps between structured objects, or a geometric/graphical construction guiding the proof.

**Goal:** Track elements, maps, and commutativity relations through a diagram to construct the required object or prove equality.

**Instructions:**
1. **Draw the Diagram Algebraically:** List objects, maps, commutative squares, exact rows/columns, and known kernels/images.
2. **Choose the Starting Element or Object:** Identify the element, path, subobject, or diagram feature to chase.
3. **Move Along Maps:** Apply maps step by step, recording where commutativity lets two paths agree.
4. **Use Exactness or Incidence:** Convert membership in a kernel to existence in an image, or use the diagram's geometric constraints.
5. **Construct the Target:** Produce the required preimage, lift, factorization, equality, or contradiction.
6. **Check Independence:** If choices were made, prove the result is independent of those choices when required.

**Common Failure Modes:**
- Chasing through a square without proving it commutes.
- Using exactness in the wrong direction.
- Confusing equality of elements with equality of maps.
