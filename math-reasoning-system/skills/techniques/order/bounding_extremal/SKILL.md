# Skill: Bounding, Monotonicity, and Extremal Objects

**Pre-condition:** Apply this skill when the proof is driven by inequalities, monotone sequences, squeeze arguments, maxima/minima, shortest or longest objects, or optimal choices.

**Goal:** Control the target by ordered comparison or choose an extremal object and show it must satisfy the desired property.

**Instructions:**
1. **Identify the Order:** Specify the partial order, metric size, degree, cardinality, norm, or energy being compared.
2. **Bounding Route:** Find lower and upper bounds, comparison objects, or a squeeze configuration.
3. **Monotonicity Route:** Prove the relevant function or sequence is increasing/decreasing and combine with boundedness or endpoint values.
4. **Extremal Route:** Choose a maximal or minimal object and prove failure of the target would allow an improvement.
5. **Close the Argument:** Translate the comparison or extremality contradiction into the desired statement.

**Common Failure Modes:**
- Assuming an extremum exists without compactness, finiteness, completeness, or Zorn-type justification.
- Missing strictness in an improvement argument.
- Using bounds that are too weak to force the target.
